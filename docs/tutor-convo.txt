### The Bare Minimum: A Single-Device Compliance Calculator The most straightforward way to address this problem is to strip away the concept of "property management" and focus entirely on the **individual device status**. --- ### The Workflow Sketch * **What the user does:** The user inputs an elevator's identifier, selects its inspection type from a dropdown menu, and types in the date of its last inspection. * **What the product does:** The engine runs a deterministic date calculation based on the selected type, compares the calculated deadline against the current date, and updates the device's status. * **The result:** A flat, single-page ledger displaying a prioritized, color-coded list where urgent deadlines bubble to the top. --- ### Why This Is Fast to Build This approach bypasses relational database complexity entirely. ### The Unconventional Solution: The "Black Box" Elevator Black Box & Civic Ledger Instead of trying to get busy human property managers to log into a dashboard and type in dates, this approach shifts the tracking burden entirely to **crowdsourced acoustics, computer vision, and public civic ledgers**. --- ### The Workflow Sketch * **The Tenant/Visitor App (Crowdsourced Ingestion):** We create a ultra-lightweight, zero-install web app (accessed via a QR code sticker pasted inside the elevator car, framed as an interactive civic game or reporting tool). * **The "Black Box" Audit:** When a tenant rides the elevator, the app uses their smartphone’s gyroscope to log ride quality, its microphone to listen for mechanical grinding sounds, and its camera to scan the physical paper inspection certificate hanging on the wall. * **The Product (The Distributed Ledger):** An AI model reads the certificate expiration date directly from the smartphone video frames. If the elevator is past its inspection window, or if the gyroscope registers dangerous acceleration drops, the system logs the data to a public, unalterable civic audit dashboard. * **The Result:** A gamified, public compliance map of the city. Property managers receive an automated alert stating: *"Tenants have flagged Elevator 3B as delinquent. You have 48 hours to log a certified update before this is automatically escalated to the city's housing authority."* --- ### Why It's Compelling It turns the traditional compliance model completely on its head. Instead of relying on a manager's internal administrative diligence, it leverages the self-interest of the people who actually use the equipment every day—the tenants. It forces compliance through radical transparency and social proof. --- ### What Makes It Impractical for a 2-Week MVP * **Hardware and Environment Chaos:** Relying on variable smartphone web APIs to capture reliable gyroscope and audio data inside a moving metal box with poor cellular service is a technical nightmare. * **The Cold Start Problem:** The software is completely useless without an active, highly motivated crowd of tenants scanning codes daily. * **Legal and Liability Risks:** Logging public, unverified claims about building safety violations opens up massive legal liabilities regarding defamation and falsified data reporting.
